Amazon Web Services - Infrastructure as a Service


Infrastructure as a Service


Infrastructure as a service is a cloud computing service where companies provide virtualised resources over the internet. It is generally used by clients as a replacement for running their own servers and infrastructure, as running servers can be costly and time consuming. Often these services will include things such as backup and security for the virtualised machines. A big advantage Iaas usually provides is a much higher capacity for processing power not normally affordable for their clients if they wished to outright purchase machines. This processing power can enable things such as big data analysis and cutting down time on processor intensive tasks such as 3d design. Ultimately, Iaas is a benefit to most businesses as it allows them to focus on their core business, rather than spending time on maintaining and operating servers and supercomputers.


Amazon Web Services


AWS refers to the range of cloud services Amazon offers. The first and probably most popular service offered is EC2, Elastic Compute Cloud 2, where clients can rent virtual systems from AWS. Another popular service is S3, which is a mass storage solution for companies. They offer many other services, such as Aurora for for databases, and Cloudfront for content delivery.
